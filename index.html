Roland E-09

How to set tones in Itende Music Style in Roland E-09?

Before everything you need to understand and be able to differentiate types of tones and when to play them and how you do you set them and finally playing them. There are tones for worship, praise, praying and preachers. So in this blog you get information on how to set tones for each specific moment in when you are in a church or event where you play itende music style. Below we start by the mostly used keyboard across south Africa when playing Itende music style, and that keyboard is Roland E-09 which is modern and also an alternative if you can’t afford or unable to get vintage Roland e-series models.

Roland E-09 Itende Basic Tone Fully Explained.

Firstly, you need set Main Tone and you do it by pressing or switching on the Tone button, secondly press or switch on the numeric button or I can say the button with text numeric written below it then now you can punch in 390 (Bass&Lead) after doing so then you need to octave your tone to negative 1 (-1), how do you do that? Press function and navigate to octave using under SELECT text arrow buttons near use program button. When you get there then the keyboard program will automatically enter into octave menu and first li is what we are looking for MAIN OCTAVE which defaults to 0 when the keyboard is switched on every time then now you need to use plus (+) button/ yes (to increase) and minus (-) no (to decrease) and for now we are going to use minus button in order to make MAIN OCTAVE to -1 that results in making the tones sound more low than high. We have done this because we are still going to set our dual Tone which is going to give use that high sound.

Secondly, you need to set Dual Tone and you do it by pressing and holding the dual button then press or switch on the numeric button or I can say the button with text numeric written above it then punch in 398 (Brightness) while holding the dual button after that then you have mixed your tones and you can release the dual button but that’s not the end we need to octave the dual tone to positive (1+), again you press function button and navigate to octave then when it automatically enters octave menu you need to navigate to the right using buttons under SELECT text, then you will see Dual OCTAVE then you need to press minus button to set it to positive one (+1).

Thirdly, you need to balance you tones volumes but in this case we need to decrease dual tone’s volume, then you press function and navigate to volumes and after automatically entering in volumes menu then navigate to the right you will see Dual volume and use yes / plus (+) button to increase and no/minus button to decrease for now we are decreasing the dual volume, decrease the volume to 75 then we are finally done setting Itende in Roland E-09.

Finally, push the modulation forward and insert a paper or something not harmful. This action is called modulating, Itende is always modulated across all keyboards it’s based on modulation actually without modulation it won’t sound as proper Itende Sound. After modulating your mixture, you can now start playing and hear how good is the Itende sound is produced by your settings you just set on your keyboard.

NOTE: THE SHORTCUT OF Inserting Dual, setting Octave and decreasing the volume is this: press & hold dual then punch in 398 while holding press yes/plus to increase dual then use buttons under the keyboards volume knob to increase or decrease the volume of dual. You can also decrease the main tone’s octave using this shortcut by pressing and holding Tone button then pressing no/minus to decrease octave to -1 and you can decrease the main tone’s volume using balance keyboard buttons under the Volume Knob. After Completing that, now press function and navigate to octave and make main tone octave -1 and make dual tone octave 0. After setting octaves then it’s time for setting volumes leave the main tone’s volume at 127 and set dual tone’s volume to 75 then that’s it, move on.

Second alternative is mixing 390 (Lead&Bass) as Main Tone Octave (-1) and mixed with 378 (Natural Ld) as Dual Octave (0) Dual Volume 75, another alternative is mixing 390 (Lead&Bass) as Main Tone Octave (-1) and mixed with 378 (Cheese Org) as Dual Octave (0) and Dual Volume 75.

Introduction into Splitting Tone before we continue for Touching Soul Tones

Splitting tones is one of the most importance things you must be able to do in your keyboard, but before we go far we need to understand what is splitting and why do we use split or why do we split tones. Splitting tones is used to play two different tone one on the right and one on the left which is quite different from dual. Splitting tones in Roland E-09 is limited because you can only split two plain tone on each side but other keyboards allow you to dual tones and split again of which is so cool. Coming back to Roland E-09 now we are going to focus on learning how to split tones, without wasting more time start by pressing and switching on main tone and press and switch on numeric button after doing so then start punching in 218 St. strings and press function and navigate to octave and make it -1 after doing so we need to press and switch on the split button then punch in 277 Violin 1 and set it’s octave to 0 or -1 then navigate to keyboard settings li then in the menu you navigate again to split point and increase to set what effect does that make after doing so then you can also play around with split hold to see which is comfortable with you. That’s the summary about splitting in Roland E-09 model.

How to set Itende

Looking at under “tone” text written in big font in the keyboard there are buttons from 1 to 9 and at the left of button number 1 there is a white button having a text Group over it, press it and switch it to A group of tones then punch in 54 Fretless Bs. But before all you need to press select and switch it to upper tone which is your main tone and lowers as your splitting tone. In Roland E-16 there is no dual tone so this means you can only play one full tone at a time except if you want to split tones then play two tone but separately but not mixed into one tone as other keyboards that allow dual function. After pressing 54 Fretless Bs. Then you have successfully set Itende in Roland E-16 and now you need to put a small pick in then modulation button (Big Triangle Button) insert the small pick at the right of the triangle button.
